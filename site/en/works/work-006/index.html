Snow (Tune: Qin Yuan Chun)

Text: Northern scenery: a thousand miles sealed in ice, ten thousand miles of drifting snow. Looking within and beyond the Great Wall, there is only boundless whiteness; up and down the great river, the surging waters suddenly fall silent. The mountains dance like silver snakes, the highlands charge like waxen elephants, all seeming to vie with heaven in height. On a clear day, wrapped in red sunlight and white snow, the land appears especially enchanting. Such a magnificent country has inspired countless heroes to bow before it. Alas, Qin Shi Huang and Han Wu were somewhat lacking in literary grace; Emperor Taizong of Tang and Emperor Taizu of Song fell a little short in poetic brilliance. Genghis Khan, that proud son of heaven, knew only how to bend his bow and shoot the great eagle. All are past. To find truly great figures, one must look to the present age.
